You can Hear the Local Orientability of an Orbifold

A mathemathematics research project on the Laplace Spectra of Riemannian Orbifolds conduceted at Lewis & Clark College advised by Dr. Liz Stanhope. This research resulted in the paper "You can Hear the Local Orientability of an Orbifold" published in the Journal "Differential Geometry and its Applications". Additionally, I have given a poster presentation and poster, and various slide presentations at Lewis & Clark College about this research project. A summary of the findings follows.

A Riemannian orbifold is a generalization of a manifold which allows for local structure given by the quotient of a Riemannian manifold with respect to finite groups of isometries. If all such isometries on the orbifold are orientation preserving, we call the orbifold locally orientable. Using heat invariants, we found that the Laplace spectrum of a Riemannian orbifold determines the local orientability of the orbifold.
